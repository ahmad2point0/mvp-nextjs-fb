# 1. What is this project?

## The one-line pitch

**CSEAS** = *Community Support and Education Aid System*. It's a website that connects three kinds of people so underprivileged students can stay in school:

- **Donors** — people with money or supplies who want to help.
- **Volunteers** — people who can give time (tutoring, delivering donations, writing reports).
- **Students** — kids/teens who need help paying for school, books, uniforms, etc.

And one more role behind the curtain:

- **Admin** — the team that verifies users, approves aid requests, and reviews donations.

## Why does this need to exist?

Imagine a student named Fatima. Her family can't afford her school fees. A kind donor named Ahmed wants to help, but he doesn't know who to give to or whether his money is being spent honestly.

CSEAS solves this. Fatima creates an aid request ("I need Rs. 5,000 for school fees"). Ahmed sees the request, donates, and gets a receipt. Sara, a volunteer, helps tutor Fatima or delivers her books. The admin verifies that everyone is who they say they are. Every step is tracked.

## What can each role actually do?

### Donor
- Make a cash donation (JazzCash, Easypaisa, or hand cash to the team).
- Or **donate items directly** (books, uniforms, food packs) — you bring them or we pick them up.
- Browse student aid requests and fund a specific one.
- Track all your donations in a dashboard.

### Volunteer
- Apply with a list of skills.
- See volunteer tasks (math tutor, donation distributor, report assistant).
- Take a task and mark it done when finished.

### Student
- Submit an aid request explaining what you need and how much it costs.
- Upload documents proving you're really a student and really need help.
- Get notified when a donor funds your request.

### Admin
- Approve or block users.
- Mark donations as verified or pending.
- See charts of total donations, students helped, tasks done.
